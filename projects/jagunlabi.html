Overview

Deception is a first-person horror game developed using Unreal Engine and C++, focused on creating an immersive and atmospheric player experience. The project explores core systems such as player interaction, environmental storytelling, and survival-based gameplay.

Built with a strong emphasis on C++ implementation, Deception serves as a hands-on project for developing scalable gameplay systems, refining technical design, and experimenting with horror mechanics used in modern game development.

What I built

Interactive Gameplay

Built interaction systems that allow players to engage with objects and progress through the world.

Puzzle & Discovery

Implemented puzzle elements that require players to interpret clues and interact with the environment.

Survival Mechanics

Designed gameplay around avoiding enemies and making strategic decisions to survive.

Narrative Clue System

Integrated a storytelling system where players discover clues through written texts to progress.

Atmospheric Horror Design

Crafted a dark and tense environment using lighting and mood to enhance fear and player immersion.

First-Person Immersion

Developed a first-person system to create a deeply immersive and personal horror experience.
